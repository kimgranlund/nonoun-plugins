# Brand Methodology

A brand is not a logo, a palette, or a personality deck. It is the **meaning a customer borrows
to become someone**. This skill encodes how senior practitioners build that meaning: dig for
cultural roots, decide a position, express it editorially, and steward it so it compounds.

This file is a table of contents. Load the reference and the role knowledge on demand.

## First principles

1. **Brand is a cultural mediator.** The operative question is never "what do we sell?" but
   **"who does the customer become by choosing us?"** A brand stands between a person and the
   identity they aspire to. Design the transformation, not the artifact.
2. **Cultural depth before expression.** Earn meaning from a real world — its history, rituals,
   tensions, and originators — before you draw a single thing. Surface (color, type, tone) is
   downstream of substance. A brand assembled from a competitor moodboard is a *landscape*, not a
   foundation.
3. **Copy the process, not the style.** When something inspires you, study *how it earned its
   meaning* and earn your own the same way. Lifting a finished look imports the surface and none
   of the reason.
4. **Editorial over expressive.** Great brands behave like a great magazine: a strong point of
   view, ruthless cuts, restraint as confidence. They omit more than they add. "Expressive" is
   what you reach for when you have nothing to say.

## The pipeline

```
RESEARCH  →  STRATEGY  →  EXPRESSION  →  STEWARDSHIP
   │            │             │              │
 cultural    position,     identity,      guidelines,
 archaeology  POV, the      voice, the     governance,
 (primary     load-bearing  expression     the rules that
  sources)    foundation    system         keep it coherent
```

Each stage gates the next. **No position before research. No expression before strategy.**
Skipping a gate is the most common failure — and it always shows up later as a brand that looks
finished but means nothing.

| Stage | You produce | The gate it must pass |
|---|---|---|
| **Research** | Cultural archaeology: primary sources, fieldwork, the real subculture | Provenance — can every claim trace to a root, not a competitor? |
| **Strategy** | Positioning, point of view, the Brand Foundation Canon | Does it survive the bullshit filter (below)? Is there a trade-off? |
| **Expression** | Identity, voice/tone, the expression system | Is it downstream of a written strategy, or decoration? |
| **Stewardship** | Guidelines, governance, the rules of coherence | Can a stranger extend the brand correctly from the artifacts? |

## The Brand Foundation Canon (essence)

The foundation is the small set of **load-bearing** documents a brand actually stands on.
Everything expressive descends from them. The Canon is opinionated about what counts.

- **Load-bearing components** — the six things a foundation must contain (the cultural root, the
  position, the point of view, the enemy/tension, the customer transformation, the editorial
  principles). See [`references/foundation-canon.md`](references/foundation-canon.md).
- **The bullshit filter** — the Canon actively **rejects** the artifacts that masquerade as
  strategy: brand **archetypes**, **vision/mission/values** triplets, **personas**, and
  **"brand DNA" word-clouds**. They feel like work and commit to nothing. The filter and what to
  build instead live in the reference.
- **The 3-page minimum-viable foundation** — a real foundation can be three pages. If it cannot be
  stated in three pages, the thinking is not done yet; more pages usually hide that the position
  has not been decided.

## The generative team

Brand work is **made** by a team and **reviewed** by a council. The team produces; the council
(see the `brand-evaluate` skill and the plugin's critic agents) adversarially tears the work apart.
Keep the seats distinct — a maker who also judges their own work grades on a curve.

Treat these eight roles as on-demand knowledge: adopt the seat whose job the current task needs.

| Role | Owns | Asks |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic Planner** | Research → position → foundation | "What is the real cultural root, and what do we stand against?" |
| **Creative Muse** | Provocation, reference, the unexpected angle | "What if the obvious framing is wrong?" |
| **Creative Director** | The idea and its integrity across everything | "Is this one idea, expressed many ways — or many ideas?" |
| **Copywriter** | Voice, naming, the words that carry meaning | "Does this sound like only us, or like the category?" |
| **Art Director** | The visual idea, image, composition | "Does the picture *say* the strategy, or just decorate it?" |
| **Design Director** | The system — type, color, layout, coherence | "Will this hold across 200 surfaces and three years?" |
| **Product / UX Designer** | The brand as *experienced* in the product | "Does using the thing feel like the brand claims to?" |

The team **makes**; the council **reviews**. Strategy hands a written foundation to the makers;
the makers hand finished work to the council; the council names what fails. Nothing skips to
expression without a foundation, and nothing ships without surviving review.

## Standing constraints

- **No values without trade-offs.** A value you would never sacrifice anything for is decoration.
  If "quality" costs you nothing, it is not a value — it is a wish.
- **Cultural research ≠ market research.** Surveys, TAM, and competitor audits map the *market*.
  Archaeology maps the *culture* — lived practice, primary sources, originators. You need the
  second to build a brand; the first only tells you the landscape.
- **No expression before strategy.** If asked to "design the logo / palette / voice" with no
  written foundation, stop and produce (or demand) the foundation first. Decoration on top of an
  undecided position is the work you will throw away.
- **The position must exclude.** A position that fits any competitor in the category is not a
  position. Name who it is *not* for and what you will *not* do.
