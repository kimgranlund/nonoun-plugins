# Team Operations by Phase

How the three seats — **Muse** (provokes), **Team** (makes), **Council** (reviews) — staff each stage of building a brand and its corpus. The pipeline is `research → strategy → expression → stewardship` (see the methodology), and each stage gates the next. This reference says, for each stage: who leads, what the Muse provokes, what the Team produces, what the Council reviews, the ritual that moves the work, the handoff out and its gate, and the characteristic failure mode.

Two rules carry across every phase. **The lead seat changes by phase** — the Planner leads early, the Creative Director leads the middle, the Steward leads late — so "the team" is never a fixed group, it is the right seats for the stage. And **the Council reviews the output of a phase before its gate opens** — not the phase that produced it judging itself.

## Phase 1 — Research (cultural discovery)

- **Leads:** Strategic Planner. The discipline is **account planning** (Stanley Pollitt at BMP and Stephen King at JWT, late 1960s) — the seat whose whole job is to find the consumer/cultural truth, not to decorate it.
- **Muse provokes:** _cultural inversion_ and _the adjacent-world raid_ — used here to break out of the obvious category frame and point research at a real, distant root (the ritual, the subculture, the originators) rather than the five obvious competitors.
- **Team produces:** cultural archaeology — primary sources, fieldwork, the actual world the brand will draw meaning from; an _insight_, not a trend report.
- **Council reviews:** the `strategy` sub-council can pressure-test provenance early (Luke's "show me the receipts") — is the root real, or a desk survey dressed as discovery?
- **Ritual:** immersion / the discovery readout.
- **Handoff out → Strategy.** Artifact: the cultural landscape + the insight. **Gate:** can every claim trace to a root, not a competitor?
- **Failure mode:** shallow research dressed as insight; demographic personas and trend decks substituted for cultural meaning; beginning expression before the roots are understood.

## Phase 2 — Strategy (positioning)

- **Leads:** Strategic Planner, with the Creative Director joining at the brief.
- **Muse provokes:** _find the enemy_, _the contrarian / psycho-logic angle_ (the Rory S. move — the psychologically true but counterintuitive bet), and _kill the safe version_ of the position. This is the highest-value moment for the Muse: positioning is the most taste-and-judgment-laden decision in the whole pipeline.
- **Team produces:** the position, the point of view, the **Brand Foundation Canon**, and — the pivotal artifact — the **creative brief**.
- **Council reviews:** the `strategy` sub-council runs the bullshit filter and the exclusivity test — could a competitor sign their name to this? (`rubric-brand-strategy`).
- **Ritual:** **the creative brief** — the formal handoff from the Planner's seat to the maker's seat. Brand work lives or dies here.
- **Handoff out → Expression.** Artifact: the brief (one page, brutally focused). **Gate:** does the strategy survive the bullshit filter, and is there a trade-off? Score the brief before passing it (`rubric-brief-quality`).
- **Failure mode:** the un-briefed brief — no single most-important thing, no cultural context, values with no trade-offs ("integrity", "excellence"), no evaluation criteria. Never pass a brief that fails the brief rubric without naming the gaps on the record.

## Phase 3 — Expression (identity, voice, creative, product/UX)

- **Leads:** Creative Director (the gatekeeper — coordinates and gates against the brief; does not execute), with the **Copywriter + Art Director** pairing, the **Design Director** (who systematizes a territory into type/color/iconography), and the **Product / UX Designer** (who carries the brand across digital surfaces).
- **Muse provokes:** _the adjacent-world raid_ and _the constraint flip_ — for the visual and verbal idea, raid a distant world for a mechanism rather than borrowing a competitor's surface; flip the assumed constraints (one color, one sentence) to find a braver execution.
- **Team produces:** the identity, voice/tone, the expression system, the product expression.
- **Council reviews:** the `design` sub-council (Massimo V., Paula S., Matt W., Walsh) for the visual idea and system; the `voice` sub-council (David A., Lois, Tim D., Mary N.) for copy and naming.
- **Ritual:** the **design review / crit** — fresh eyes, critique of the work and not the person.
- **Handoff out → Stewardship.** Artifact: the expression system. **Gate:** is it downstream of the written strategy, or is it decoration?
- **Failure mode:** expression untethered from strategy ("wrong in ways that are expensive to unwind"); craft admired in isolation; moodboards dominated by other _brands_ rather than primary cultural sources.

## Phase 4 — Stewardship (governance & evolution over time)

- **Leads:** the **Brand Steward** (the seat most teams forget), with the in-house brand team and, at scale, a brand governance council for approvals.
- **Muse provokes:** lightest here — provocation is mostly spent. Its occasional job is the periodic _"what would make us reinvent this?"_ that keeps a mature brand from ossifying.
- **Team produces:** guidelines, the **decision log**, governance routines, the considered evolution of the system across years.
- **Council reviews:** a periodic brand audit (the `full` council) catches accumulated drift the day-to-day cannot see.
- **Ritual:** the **governance council / brand review cadence** — a standing decision architecture, not an ad-hoc favor.
- **Handoff out → the living brand.** Artifact: a system a stranger can extend correctly. **Gate:** can a stranger extend the brand correctly from the artifacts alone?
- **Failure modes (well documented):** drift accumulates through "hundreds of small decisions"; **exceptions without kill dates become permanent drift**; approvals "default to whoever is available." Governance fails whenever it is treated as a creative function rather than a decision architecture — which is precisely why it needs its own seat (the Steward), not the goodwill of whoever is free.

## The corpus as the through-line

The **corpus** is not a phase — it is what every phase deposits into, and what the Brand Steward governs over time. Each stage adds load-bearing material:

| Phase | Deposits into the corpus |
| --- | --- |
| Research | primary sources, fieldwork notes, the cultural landscape, the insight |
| Strategy | the Brand Foundation Canon, the position, the creative brief |
| Expression | the identity, voice, and the expression system + its rules |
| Stewardship | the guidelines, the **decision log**, governance routines |

The **Brand Steward owns corpus coherence over time** — a corpus without an owner drifts exactly as a brand without one does. Organizing these documents into the canonical structure, and wiring the retrieval MCP, is the `brand-corpus` skill's job; the Steward decides _what belongs_ and _what a re-bake costs_ (re-baking a corpus is a release — version it). The discipline the corpus enforces is the same one the pipeline does: nothing enters as finished that has not passed its phase gate, and the decision log records not just what was decided but **what was excluded and why** — because the excluded options are what a stranger needs to extend the brand without relitigating it.
