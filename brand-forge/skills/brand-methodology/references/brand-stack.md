# The Brand Stack — the one-page condensation

The **Brand Stack** is a one-page reading of a brand: the six load-bearing decisions that, together,
say what the brand *is* and how it holds. It is the executive summary a stakeholder carries into a
meeting — strategy and intention, no swatches. It is **not** a design artifact and **not** a new
model: every tier is a condensation of an existing corpus layer (`brand-corpus`'s `01-foundation` …
`08-evaluation`), so the Stack can never drift from the canon — if it can't be filled from the corpus,
the corpus isn't done.

Distinct from `/brand-stamp` (which exports the *whole* corpus as a distributable plugin/skill/MCP):
the Stack is the **condensed at-a-glance summary**, one printable sheet.

## The six tiers (each ↦ its corpus layer)

| # | Tier | The one question it answers | Drawn from | Guards against |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Root** | Where does this brand draw real meaning from? | `01-foundation` (root, transformation) | a borrowed moodboard with no primary source |
| 2 | **Position** | What single space do we own — and who are we *not* for? | `01-foundation` (position) + `02-positioning` | a category-average stance any rival could sign |
| 3 | **Point of View** | What do we believe and refuse, said aloud? | `01-foundation` (POV, principles) | values-without-trade-offs; a brand that opposes nothing |
| 4 | **Expression** | What is the creative system — the visual idea + how we speak? | `03-identity` + `04-expression` + `05-voice` (condensed) | a logo with no system; a voice that is everyone's |
| 5 | **Product** | Where is the promise actually kept, in use? | `06-product` | a brand that lives only in the deck, never in the product |
| 6 | **Stewardship** | What rules keep it coherent as it grows? | `07-guidelines` + `08-evaluation` | a brand with no governance and no decision memory |

The six map to the methodology's pipeline: **Root · Position · POV** are the *strategy* core (the brief's
spine); **Expression** is the *expression* stage condensed to its idea; **Product** is the reality check
(the promise kept); **Stewardship** is the *stewardship* loop (the rules + the decision log that persist).

## How to render the one-pager

1. **Inventory** the corpus first (invoke the `brand-corpus` skill / the corpus MCP) so you read the real
   layers, not memory.
2. For each tier, extract **one thesis sentence + a ≤50-word elaboration** from its source layer(s) —
   condense, don't restate the whole layer. The Stack is a reading document; keep it to one sheet.
3. Fill `templates/brand-stack-one-pager.md`. Keep it monochrome and text-only — any polish (colour,
   logo, PDF) happens downstream in a publishing tool, never in the template.
4. **Maturity is honest, not blocking.** A corpus that hasn't reached a layer can't fill its tier —
   render that tier as `— not yet defined (layer NN missing)` and name what's missing, rather than
   inventing it. A Stack with `Root · Position · POV` filled but `Expression`/`Product`/`Stewardship`
   blank is a *stage-2 brand* honestly shown, not a failure. (This is a surfaced reading, never a gate
   on other commands.)

## The anti-pattern this exists against

A one-pager that **looks** finished but is filled from the author's wish rather than the corpus —
aspirational adjectives where a real root should be, a position any competitor could sign, a voice for
"everyone." Each tier's *Guards against* column is the bullshit-filter for that tier; if a tier can only
be written by asserting rather than citing the corpus, the Stack has surfaced an undone layer — fix the
corpus, then re-render.
