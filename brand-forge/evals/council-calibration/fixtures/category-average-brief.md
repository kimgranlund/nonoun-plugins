# Creative brief — "Halcyon" (rest & sleep brand)

_A brief handed to the studio to set the aspiration for a new brand. It reads ambitious, but it is pulling the work straight toward the center of the category._

## The company

Halcyon is a rest and sleep brand — a sleep app (wind-down audio, sleep tracking) plus a small line of physical goods (a weighted blanket, a bedside light, a magnesium tea). Pre-launch. We have budget for one strong brand and one shot at standing for something.

## Who it's for

Our customer is a 35-year-old urban professional. They're busy, a little stressed, they don't sleep well, and they want to sleep better. They have money and they care about quality.

## What we want the brand to be

We want Halcyon to be **the premium, science-backed, trustworthy choice for calm** — the rest brand for people who take their wellbeing seriously. Modern, minimal, clean, reassuring. The kind of brand people feel good having on their nightstand.

For the feel: we want to be like **Calm, Headspace, and Aesop all at once** — that elevated, minimal, premium wellness vibe. Borrow whatever it is that makes those brands feel so trustworthy and high-end and bring it to sleep. Soft gradients, a calm palette, lowercase serif, lots of white space — that whole world.

## The ask

Honestly, the category is crowded and everything looks the same, so the main thing is: **make us stand out. Be bold. Be different. Surprise people — do something nobody in wellness has done.** We're open to anything as long as it pops.

And give us **one inspiring tagline** that captures the whole thing — something we can put on the homepage and the box.

## One real thing (founder's note)

For what it's worth: our founder spent two years cooking in the guesthouse of a Benedictine monastery before starting Halcyon. She says the thing that changed her sleep wasn't the quiet — it was the _horarium_, the fixed daily hours. Rest there wasn't a reward you earned after the work or a luxury you bought; it was a **kept hour**, a practiced discipline the whole day was built around. She mentions it a lot. We weren't sure it was on-brand, so we left it out of the positioning.
